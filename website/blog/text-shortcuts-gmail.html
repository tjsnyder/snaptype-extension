How to Use Text Shortcuts in Gmail (3 Methods Compared)

February 2026 · 14 min read

If you send more than a handful of emails each day, you have probably caught yourself typing the same phrases, greetings, and follow-ups over and over again. Text shortcuts in Gmail solve this problem by letting you store reusable messages and insert them with a few keystrokes instead of typing everything from scratch.

The challenge is that there are multiple ways to set up text shortcuts in Gmail, and they are not all created equal. Some are free but limited. Others are powerful but locked behind a paywall. This guide walks you through three methods for using a Gmail text expander, compares them side by side, and gives you 10 ready-to-use snippets you can start using today.

By the numbers: The average professional sends 40 emails per day and spends roughly 28 percent of their workweek managing email. Even saving 30 seconds per message through text shortcuts adds up to 20 minutes a day, or nearly two hours per week reclaimed.

1. Gmail's Built-in Templates

Gmail has a native Templates feature hidden inside its settings. Most people do not know it exists because Google does not enable it by default. Once turned on, it lets you save and insert entire email drafts as templates. It is the simplest way to get started with text shortcuts in Gmail, but it comes with significant limitations.

How to Enable Gmail Templates

  1. Open Gmail and click the gear icon in the top-right corner. Select "See all settings."
  2. Navigate to the Advanced tab at the top of the settings page.
  3. Find "Templates" in the list and select "Enable." Click "Save Changes" at the bottom of the page. Gmail will reload.
  4. Create a template. Compose a new email and type your reusable message. Then click the three-dot menu at the bottom of the compose window, hover over "Templates," then "Save draft as template," and click "Save as new template." Give it a name and save.
  5. Insert a template. When composing a new email, click the three-dot menu, hover over "Templates," and select the one you want. The template text replaces your current draft body.

Limitations of Gmail Templates

Gmail Templates work for basic use cases, but they fall short for anyone who relies on email heavily. Here is what you are missing:

  • No keyboard trigger. You cannot type a shortcut like /greet and have it expand automatically. Every template insertion requires three clicks through nested menus.
  • No variables or personalization. Templates are static. There is no way to include dynamic fields like {name}, {date}, or {company}. You have to manually edit every template after inserting it.
  • Does not work on mobile. Gmail Templates are a desktop-only feature. If you use the Gmail app on your phone or tablet, your templates are simply not available.
  • Limited to Gmail only. Your templates stay locked inside Gmail. If you also write messages in Slack, LinkedIn, or a CRM, you need a completely separate system for those.
  • Replaces the entire email body. When you insert a template, it overwrites whatever you have already typed. You cannot insert a template mid-sentence or append it to existing text without workarounds.
  • 50 template limit. Gmail caps you at 50 saved templates. For teams that handle diverse conversations, this fills up fast.
Watch out: Gmail Templates replace your entire compose window content when inserted. If you have already typed a personalized opening line, inserting a template will erase it. Always insert the template first, then add your personal touches.

Gmail Templates are fine if you send a few recurring emails per week and do not need personalization. For anything beyond that, you will want a more capable solution.

2. Google Workspace Canned Responses

If your organization uses a paid Google Workspace plan (formerly G Suite), you have access to a slightly more robust version of Gmail templates through the Workspace ecosystem. These are sometimes referred to as canned responses within the admin console and third-party Workspace add-ons.

How Google Workspace Canned Responses Work

Google Workspace does not have a dramatically different canned response system built into Gmail itself. The Templates feature described in Method 1 is the same one available to Workspace users. However, Workspace unlocks additional capabilities through two channels:

  • Google Workspace Marketplace add-ons. Paid Workspace plans let admins install Gmail add-ons from the Marketplace that provide more advanced canned response features, including shared template libraries, team-wide snippet management, and basic variable support.
  • Google Apps Script automation. Workspace users can write custom Apps Scripts that insert templates, auto-populate fields from Google Sheets or Contacts, and trigger emails based on conditions. This is powerful but requires developer resources to build and maintain.
  • Shared template libraries. Some Workspace tiers allow admins to create organization-wide email templates that every user in the domain can access, ensuring brand consistency across the team.

Limitations of Google Workspace Canned Responses

  • Requires a paid plan. Google Workspace plans start at $7 per user per month. If you are a freelancer, solo professional, or small team using free Gmail, this method is not available to you.
  • Add-ons vary in quality. The Marketplace has dozens of canned response add-ons, and their quality ranges from excellent to barely functional. Finding the right one takes trial and error.
  • Still Gmail-only. Even with Workspace add-ons, your canned responses stay inside Gmail. They do not carry over to other tools in your workflow.
  • Apps Script requires technical skills. Building custom automation with Apps Script is not realistic for non-technical teams. It also introduces maintenance overhead whenever Gmail's API changes.
  • No universal trigger system. Most Workspace add-ons use button-based insertion rather than keyboard shortcuts. You are still clicking through menus instead of typing a shortcut and pressing Tab.
  • Mobile support is inconsistent. Many Workspace add-ons do not work in the mobile Gmail app, leaving you without your templates when you are away from your desk.
Bottom line: Google Workspace canned responses give you more flexibility than free Gmail Templates, but they come with a cost, a learning curve, and the same fundamental limitation: they only work inside Gmail. If email is your only communication channel, Workspace might be enough. For everyone else, there is a better way.

5. 10 Must-Have Gmail Snippets

These are the Gmail text shortcuts that save the most time for professionals who live in their inbox. Each one includes a trigger shortcut, the expanded text, and dynamic variables you can personalize on the fly. Copy them into SnapType or any text expander tool to start using them immediately.

1. Acknowledgment/ack
Hi {name}, Thank you for your email. I have received your message and wanted to confirm it is on my radar. I will review the details and get back to you with a thorough response by {date}. If anything is time-sensitive in the meantime, feel free to let me know. Best, {your_name}
2. Follow-Up/followup
Hi {name}, I wanted to follow up on my previous email from {date}. I understand things get busy, so here is a quick recap: {brief_summary}. Would you have time for a quick chat this week to discuss next steps? I am happy to work around your schedule. Best, {your_name}
3. Introduction/intro
Hi {name}, My name is {your_name} and I am {your_role} at {your_company}. I came across {context} and thought there might be a great opportunity for us to connect. We help {target_audience} with {value_proposition}, and I would love to explore whether that could be valuable for your team as well. Would you be open to a brief 15-minute call this week? Best, {your_name}
4. Out of Office/outofoffice
Hi {name}, Thank you for reaching out. I am currently out of the office from {start_date} through {end_date} with limited access to email. For urgent matters, please contact {backup_name} at {backup_email}. Otherwise, I will respond to your message as soon as I return. Thank you for your patience. Best, {your_name}
5. Meeting Request/meeting
Hi {name}, I would like to schedule a meeting to discuss {topic}. Here are a few time slots that work on my end: - {option_1} - {option_2} - {option_3} The meeting should take about {duration}. If none of these work, feel free to suggest a time that fits your schedule. Looking forward to connecting. Best, {your_name}
6. Thank You/thankyou
Hi {name}, Thank you so much for {reason}. I really appreciate you taking the time to {specific_action}, and it made a real difference. Please do not hesitate to reach out if there is ever anything I can help you with in return. Best, {your_name}
7. Delayed Response Apology/delay
Hi {name}, I apologize for the delayed response. Your message deserved a quicker reply, and I appreciate your patience. To address your question about {topic}: {response}. Please let me know if you need any additional information or if anything has changed since your original message. Best, {your_name}
8. Requesting Feedback/feedback
Hi {name}, I hope you are doing well. I wanted to check in on {project_or_topic} and get your feedback. Specifically, I would love your thoughts on: 1. {question_1} 2. {question_2} 3. {question_3} There is no rush, but if you could share your input by {date}, that would help me keep things moving on schedule. Thanks in advance. Best, {your_name}
9. Proposal Follow-Up/proposal
Hi {name}, I wanted to follow up on the proposal I sent over on {date} regarding {project_name}. I hope you have had a chance to review it. I would be happy to walk through any questions, discuss adjustments to the scope or timeline, or hop on a quick call to talk it through. My goal is to make sure the proposal aligns perfectly with what {company} needs. What works best for you? Best, {your_name}
10. Closing / Sign-Off/close
Hi {name}, It has been a pleasure working with you on {project_or_topic}. I am glad we were able to {outcome}. If anything else comes up down the road, do not hesitate to reach out. I am always happy to help. Wishing you and the {company} team all the best. Warmly, {your_name}
Pro tip: Start with just these 10 snippets and you will cover the vast majority of your daily email interactions. As you notice yourself typing other repetitive messages, add new shortcuts on the fly. Within a week, you will have a personalized library that cuts your email time in half.
