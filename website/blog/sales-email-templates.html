30 Sales Email Templates That Get Replies

February 2026 · 7 min read

The best salespeople don't write every email from scratch. They use proven templates as a starting point and personalize from there. Here are 30 templates organized by stage of the sales cycle, each with a suggested SnapType shortcut.

Key principle: Templates are starting points, not scripts. Always personalize the first line and any references to the prospect's company or situation.

Cold Outreach

Value-first introduction/cold1
Hi {name}, I noticed {company} is [specific observation about their business]. We help similar companies [specific result], and I thought there might be a fit. Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call this week?
Mutual connection/cold2
Hi {name}, [Mutual connection] mentioned you might be interested in [topic]. We've helped companies like [similar company] achieve [result]. Would it make sense to chat briefly about how we could help {company}?
Problem-focused/cold3
Hi {name}, Most [role/industry] leaders I talk to are dealing with [common pain point]. We've built a solution that [how you solve it], and teams like [reference company] have seen [specific result]. Worth a 10-minute conversation?

Follow-Ups

Gentle nudge (3 days)/fu1
Hi {name}, Just bumping this to the top of your inbox. I'd love to show you how we can help {company} with [value prop]. Would any of these times work? - [Time 1] - [Time 2]
Add value (1 week)/fu2
Hi {name}, I came across [relevant article/resource] and thought of {company}. It covers [topic] and has some useful insights. Also wanted to circle back on my earlier note — still happy to chat if the timing works.
Breakup email (2 weeks)/breakup
Hi {name}, I've reached out a few times and haven't heard back. I don't want to be a pest, so this will be my last email. If things change in the future, I'm always happy to chat. Wishing you and the {company} team all the best.

After a Meeting

Meeting recap/recap
Hi {name}, Great speaking with you today! Here's a quick recap of what we discussed: - [Key point 1] - [Key point 2] - [Key point 3] Next steps: [agreed action items] I'll follow up on {date} as we discussed. In the meantime, don't hesitate to reach out with any questions.
Send resources/resources
Hi {name}, As promised, here are the resources I mentioned: 1. [Resource name] — [link] 2. [Resource name] — [link] 3. [Case study] — [link] Let me know if you have any questions as you review these. Looking forward to our next conversation on [date].

Objection Handling

Price objection/objprice
Completely understand the budget concern, {name}. A few data points that might help: - [Client A] saw [X]% ROI within [timeframe] - The average payback period for our customers is [period] - We also offer [flexible payment/annual discount] Would it be helpful to walk through a custom ROI analysis for {company}?
Timing objection/objtime
Totally hear you on the timing. Many of our best customers actually started during similar periods — getting ahead of [season/initiative] gave them a head start. What if we set up a lightweight pilot so you can see results before committing fully? No pressure to expand until you're ready.
Competitor comparison/objcomp
[Competitor] is a solid product. The main differences our customers highlight are: 1. [Key differentiator 1] 2. [Key differentiator 2] 3. [Key differentiator 3] Happy to do a side-by-side comparison tailored to {company}'s specific needs if that would help.

Closing

Welcome new customer/welcome
Welcome aboard, {name}! We're thrilled to have {company} as a customer. Here's what happens next: 1. Your account is being set up (ETA: [timeframe]) 2. Your dedicated account manager [name] will reach out within 24 hours 3. We'll schedule a kickoff call to align on goals and timeline Thanks for choosing us — we're excited to get started!

Tips for Better Sales Emails

  • Personalize the first line. Reference something specific about the prospect or their company. Generic openings get ignored.
  • Keep it short. Aim for 3-5 sentences in cold emails. Respect their time and they'll respect yours.
  • One CTA per email. Don't ask them to read a case study AND book a call AND watch a video. Pick one action.
  • Send at the right time. Tuesday through Thursday, 8-10 AM in their timezone, tends to get the best open rates.
  • Follow up. 80% of sales require 5+ follow-ups, but 44% of reps give up after one. Be persistent without being pushy.
