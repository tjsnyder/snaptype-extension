How to Save 2 Hours Every Week with Text Shortcuts

February 2026 · 5 min read

Most knowledge workers type the same phrases dozens of times per day without realizing it. Email greetings, meeting follow-ups, support responses, status updates — the repetition adds up fast.

104 hours
saved per year at just 2 hours/week

Text shortcuts (also called text expansion) replace this repetition with a simple system: type a short abbreviation, press a key, and your full text appears. Here's how to set it up effectively.

The 5-Step System

1

Audit your typing for one day

Pay attention to every message, email, and comment you write. When you notice yourself typing something you've typed before, write it down. Most people identify 10-20 repeated phrases in a single day.

2

Categorize by frequency

Sort your list by how often you type each phrase. The ones you use 5+ times per day go at the top. These are your highest-value shortcuts — each one saves you the most cumulative time.

3

Create memorable shortcuts

Use a consistent prefix (like /) followed by a short, logical abbreviation. /hello for your greeting. /sig for your signature. /meet for meeting follow-ups. The shortcut should be obvious enough that you never forget it.

4

Add dynamic variables

Make your templates smarter by using variables. Instead of a static date, use {date}. Instead of your name, use {name}. This way your shortcuts produce personalized output every time.

5

Build the habit over two weeks

Start with your top 5 shortcuts. Use them for two weeks until they're muscle memory. Then add 5 more. Within a month, you'll have 15-20 shortcuts that save you hours every week without thinking about it.

Common shortcuts that save the most time

Highest-ROI shortcuts: Email greetings, email signatures, meeting follow-ups, support responses, status update templates, and common code patterns.

For everyone

  • /hello — Standard greeting
  • /sig — Email signature with name, title, contact info
  • /thanks — Thank you response
  • /busy — "I'm tied up, will get back to you by..."
  • /meet — Meeting follow-up template

For support teams

  • /ack — Acknowledge receipt of a ticket
  • /details — Request more information
  • /resolved — Close a resolved ticket
  • /escalate — Escalation notice

For salespeople

  • /cold — Cold outreach template
  • /followsale — Follow-up after no response
  • /demo — Schedule a demo
  • /proposal — Proposal follow-up

Measuring your time savings

A good text expander tracks your usage. After a week, check your stats: how many expansions you've triggered and how many keystrokes you've saved. Most users are surprised to find they've saved thousands of keystrokes in just the first few days.

At an average typing speed of 40 WPM, saving 5,000 keystrokes per week translates to roughly 25 minutes. Scale that up with more shortcuts and longer templates, and 2+ hours per week is very achievable.
